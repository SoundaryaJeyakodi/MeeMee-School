Elementary School (Grades K-5)

Our elementary school program focuses on building a strong foundation in core subjects while fostering creativity and social development.

  • English Language Arts: Literacy, reading comprehension, writing, and vocabulary building.
  • Mathematics: Number sense, basic operations, geometry, and problem-solving skills.
  • Science: Hands-on exploration of nature, basic biology, earth science, and the scientific method.
  • Social Studies: Introduction to history, geography, and communities.
  • Art & Music: Encouraging self-expression through visual and performing arts.
  • Physical Education: Developing coordination, teamwork, and healthy habits.

    • Middle School (Grades 6-8)

    Our middle school program helps students transition from elementary to high school by offering a deeper dive into academic subjects while providing opportunities for personal growth.

  • English Literature & Writing:Advanced reading comprehension, essay writing, and creative writing.
  • Mathematics: Pre-algebra, algebra, geometry, and critical thinking.
  • Science: Physical science, life science, environmental studies, and lab experiments.
  • Social Studies: U.S. history, world geography, and civic education.
  • Technology: Basic coding, digital literacy, and computer skills.
  • World Languages: Introduction to languages such as Spanish, French, or Mandarin.
  • Electives: Art, music, drama, and more.
  • Health & Physical Education: Fitness, health education, and team sports.

    • High School (Grades 9-12)

    Our high school curriculum prepares students for college, careers, and beyond, offering a variety of classes and pathways to support different academic and personal goals.

  • English: American and world literature, creative writing, and research writing.
  • Mathematics: Algebra I & II, geometry, trigonometry, calculus, and statistics.
  • Science : Biology, chemistry, physics, environmental science, and AP science courses.
  • Social Studies: World history, U.S. government, economics, and psychology.
  • World Languages: Spanish, French, Mandarin, and other languages.
  • Technology & Computer Science: Coding, app development, web design, and computer science principles.
  • Fine Arts: Visual arts, theater, music theory, and digital media.
  • Physical Education & Health: Personal fitness, sports training, and health awareness.
  • Electives: A wide range of electives including robotics, journalism, photography, business studies, and culinary arts.
  • Advanced Placement (AP): College-level courses in subjects such as math, science, history, and languages.

    • Special Programs

    Special programs designed to enrich our students' learning experiences and foster their unique talents

  • STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Mathematics): Focused classes and extracurricular activities that promote problem-solving, critical thinking, and innovation in STEM fields.
  • Gifted & Talented Program: Enrichment opportunities for advanced learners, including special projects and accelerated coursework.
  • Special Education: Customized support for students with learning differences, ensuring they receive personalized attention and resources.
  • Extracurricular Activities: A variety of clubs and sports that encourage leadership, teamwork, and personal growth outside the classroom.
